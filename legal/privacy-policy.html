Shoogar Soft Inc. ("Shoogar Soft", "we", "us", "our") respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, who we share it with, and the rights you have over it.

This policy is written to comply with Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and Alberta's Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA). Because we are an Alberta organization handling information largely within Alberta, Alberta PIPA generally governs our activities. Because some of our processors are located outside Canada (in the United States), PIPEDA's rules on cross-border transfers also apply to those transfers. This policy addresses both.

1. What Personal Information We Collect

We collect only the information we need to deliver our service. Specifically:

1.1 From the intake form (Tally.so)

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Hosting target (the hardware or VPS you want the system installed on, and related technical details you choose to provide)
  • Accounts you want connected (for example, which email, accounting, or repository accounts you intend to link)
  • Your main goal for the system

1.2 When you contact us

  • Email content and email address when you write to claude@shoogarsoft.com or otherwise correspond with us.

1.3 Payment information

When you pay, Stripe collects and processes your payment details (such as card information and billing details). We do not receive or store your full card number. We receive limited confirmation and transaction information from Stripe (for example, that a payment succeeded, the amount, and a reference identifier) for accounting and tax purposes.

1.4 Account credentials provided for setup

During delivery you may provide credentials, app passwords, or access tokens so we can configure your instance. We treat these as highly sensitive. We use them only to perform the configuration, and we ask you to rotate or revoke any shared credentials after handoff (see Section 5).

We do not collect special categories of sensitive personal information (such as health, biometric, or government-ID data) as part of this service, and we ask you not to send them.

2. Why We Collect It (Purpose)

We collect and use your personal information for service delivery only, specifically to:

  • Communicate with you about your engagement;
  • Understand your goals and configure your instance accordingly;
  • Connect the accounts you have asked us to connect;
  • Process your payment and issue receipts/invoices;
  • Meet our legal, accounting, and tax-record obligations.

We do not use your personal information for advertising, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to delivering and supporting the service you purchased.

3. Consent

By submitting the intake form, contacting us, or purchasing the service, you consent to the collection, use, and disclosure of your personal information as described in this policy. You may withdraw your consent at any time (see Section 8), subject to legal or contractual restrictions and reasonable notice; withdrawing consent may mean we can no longer deliver or support the service.

3.1 Email communications (CASL)

Email we send you in connection with your purchase and delivery (confirmations, clarifying questions, the handoff document, and support) is transactional and relationship-based correspondence. If we ever send you commercial or promotional email, it will identify us and include a working unsubscribe mechanism, and we will honour unsubscribe requests within 10 business days, consistent with Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL).

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share it with third parties for their own marketing or any unrelated purpose.

4.1 Cross-border transfers

Because Tally.so and Stripe operate outside Canada, your personal information may be stored or processed in other countries (including the United States and the European Union). While stored or processed in another country, your information may be subject to that country's laws, including lawful access by foreign courts, law enforcement, and government authorities. By using our service you acknowledge this transfer. We use service providers that maintain commercially reasonable security and contractual protections for the data they process on our behalf.

5. How Long We Keep It (Retention)

  • Service and correspondence data (intake responses, emails, configuration notes): retained for the duration of your engagement and for a reasonable period afterward to support you.
  • Financial and transaction records (invoices, receipts, payment confirmations): retained for seven (7) years to meet Canadian tax and corporate record-keeping requirements.
  • Account credentials you shared for setup: used only during configuration and deleted from our working environment after handoff. We ask you to rotate or revoke any shared credentials once delivery is complete, so that no live credential of yours remains in our possession.

When personal information is no longer needed for these purposes and is not subject to a retention obligation, we securely delete or anonymize it.

6. How We Protect It

We apply security measures appropriate to the sensitivity of the information, including:

  • Limiting access to personal information to those who need it to deliver the service (in practice, the founder);
  • Transmitting sensitive credentials over secure channels and avoiding unnecessary copies;
  • Storing records on access-controlled systems;
  • Deleting credentials from our working environment after handoff.

No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure. While we take reasonable steps to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Use of AI

We use AI tools (large language models, including the Anthropic Claude API) to deliver and operate the service. Where AI processing involves your personal information, that processing is limited to the service-delivery purposes described in this policy. We do not authorize our AI providers to use your personal information to train their general models beyond what is necessary to provide the service, consistent with those providers' terms.

8. Your Rights

Under PIPEDA and Alberta PIPA, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you;
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete personal information;
  • Withdraw consent to our use of your personal information (subject to legal/contractual limits);
  • Request deletion of your personal information, subject to our legal obligation to retain financial records for seven years (Section 5);
  • Ask questions or make a complaint about how we handle your personal information.

To exercise any of these rights, email claude@shoogarsoft.com. We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law (generally within 30 days under PIPEDA/PIPA). We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact:

  • Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner of Alberta — oipc.ab.ca
  • Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada — priv.gc.ca

9. Children

The service is intended for businesses and adults. We do not knowingly collect personal information from individuals under the age of majority. If you believe a minor has provided us personal information, contact us and we will delete it.
